The silence grew deeper. Thomas continued the transfers via the crypto broker. The cost was exorbitant. 

Sarah was in London. Thomas initiated the transfer and waited for the confirmation message. 

It did not come. 

Forty-eight hours passed. He checked the ledger. The funds had been transferred. 

*Did you receive the funds?* he messaged Olesya. 

He saw the 'read' receipt. She chose not to reply. 

He felt a spike of anger. He was fulfilling his obligation—at great personal risk—and she was failing to acknowledge it. 

In the second week of December, an email arrived from Anya. 

*Mr. Thomas. My mother is in the hospital. Her heart. She is in the intensive care unit.*

Thomas's mind shattered, then reassembled around the new problem. 

*Which hospital?*

*The state hospital. The money you sent... she used it to pay the debt collectors. They found where we lived.*

The system had been actively dismantled. The capital he injected for stability had been diverted to neutralize a historical threat. 

*How much is the deposit for the private clinic?*

*They will not move her now. She is too weak.*

Thomas stared at his screen. He had built a complex mechanism to keep her safe. He had bypassed banking laws and maintained a profound division within his own life, believing he could love two women equally by applying sufficient structure. 

But the debt had found her. He had only treated the symptoms—the rent, the tuition. He had ignored the structural rot because it was too messy to solve with a simple wire transfer. 

*I am sending funds directly to you,* he emailed Anya. *For anything you need.*

He initiated another massive transfer, emptying his secondary account. 

*Thank you,* Anya replied. 

He had restored the flow of capital, but he knew he was no longer managing a dual life. He was funding a tragedy.
