The updates from Anya were clinical. 

*She is stable but unconscious.*

Thomas responded with instructions to spare no expense. He was operating on reflex. 

Sarah returned from London. "You're distracted," she said. "Is it the new pipeline?"

"Yes," Thomas lied. "Complex routing issues."

Two weeks later, Anya emailed. 

*She is awake. She wants to go home.*

*Tell the doctors I will cover the cost of a private nurse,* Thomas replied. 

*She says she does not want a nurse. She just wants to be home.*

A week after Olesya went home, Thomas received a message on the secure app. 

*Thomas. I am home.*

*I have sent funds to cover the recovery period,* he typed. 

*I used the money you sent before to pay the debt. The men came. They threatened Anya.*

*I understand. The debt is cleared.*

*It is not cleared, Thomas. They added interest. They will come back.*

The debt was a living entity consuming the resources he sent. 

*How much is the total?* he asked. 

*It does not matter. It is more than you can send.*

*Give me the number, Olesya.*

She did not reply. 

The Delhi assignment was concluding. The company approved their transfer to Norway. 

Thomas realized remote maintenance was no longer viable. The distance was preventing him from fixing the core problem. If he truly believed his love was equal, he had to integrate it into reality. He had to close the distance. 

He began researching Norwegian immigration law, specifically the requirements for sponsoring a specialized worker visa. He would extract her. It was the ultimate logistical solution to an ideological paradox.
