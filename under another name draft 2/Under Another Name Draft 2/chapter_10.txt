The sporadic emergency transfers coalesced into a fixed monthly stipend. On the first of every month, Thomas would authorize a wire. 

With the financial panic neutralized, the messages shifted. 

*Anya finished her exams today.*

*The new apartment has reliable heating.*

Thomas read these messages during his breaks in the compound. He rarely replied with more than a sentence. He did not want details. He was managing a remote subsidiary. 

Sarah arrived in the compound. She quickly established a routine, organizing her remote work. 

One evening, Sarah reviewed their joint accounts. "We have a significant surplus. We should consider shifting it into the index funds."

"Agreed," Thomas said. 

"Your personal account seems lower than usual," Sarah noted. 

"Equipment upgrades," he lied smoothly. 

The lie was functional. He realized that the system with Olesya was the only area of his life that was entirely his own. His marriage to Sarah was a partnership of transparency. The Baku arrangement was a closed loop where he was the sole authority. 

A week later, Olesya messaged him.

*Today is my birthday.*

He opened his banking app, initiated a supplementary transfer, and typed a reference note: *For dinner.*

He sent the confirmation screenshot. *Happy Birthday.*

*You did not have to do this,* she replied. 

*It is for dinner,* he reiterated. 

He had provided the resource to facilitate the event. In Olesya's mind, the dinner was a celebration provided by a partner. In Thomas's mind, it was a successful execution of a logistical parameter. 

The distance insulated him from the reality that she was waiting for him to return, building a life around the expectation of his eventual physical presence. He believed the capital itself was the expression of love.
