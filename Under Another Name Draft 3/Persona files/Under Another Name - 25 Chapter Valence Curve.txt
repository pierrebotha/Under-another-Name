UNDER ANOTHER NAME - 25 CHAPTER VALENCE CURVE

Target build: 25 chapters at approximately 5,000 words each
Projected manuscript length: approximately 125,000 words

Purpose:
This curve is designed to make the novel emotionally engaging without turning it into melodrama. The movement should feel like a system working beautifully, then losing pressure, then exposing the human cost of being treated as a system.

Valence scale:
+5 = warmth, hope, intimacy, apparent stability
0 = controlled neutrality, routine, system maintenance
-5 = collapse, loss, exposure, emotional void

Tension scale:
1 = low pressure
10 = maximum pressure

Governing emotional shape:
The novel should begin in controlled restraint, rise through the seductive comfort of provision, hold a long plateau of functioning duality, then decline in stages as each system fails. The emotional experience should not be a straight fall. It should pulse: relief after each payment, hope after each new mechanism, then deeper damage when the mechanism proves inadequate.

The reader should feel the attraction of Thomas's logic before seeing its cruelty.


PART I: ORIGIN - BAKU

Chapter 01 - The First System
Target valence: +1
Target tension: 3
Emotional movement:
From professional neutrality into quiet curiosity.
Thomas arrives in Baku and treats the city, the rigs, and the office as systems to be understood. Olesya enters as an apparent administrative weakness, but the attraction begins in what she does control: space, order, domestic precision.
Engagement function:
The reader should feel the first contradiction. She is bad at the job, but exact with the world around her. Thomas notices the wrong thing and mistakes that noticing for efficiency.
Ending pressure:
He corrects her files after she leaves. The room is orderly because of her. The documents are correct because of him.

Chapter 02 - Compensation
Target valence: +2
Target tension: 4
Emotional movement:
From practical correction into private provision.
Thomas begins absorbing Olesya's failures and applying resources quietly. Cash appears. Problems vanish. Olesya understands the arrangement before he does.
Engagement function:
The reader should enjoy the elegance of the system while sensing the moral danger in its silence.
Ending pressure:
The money disappears from her drawer without a word. The next morning she arrives on time.

Chapter 03 - The Negative Space
Target valence: +3
Target tension: 4
Emotional movement:
From proximity into unspoken intimacy.
Their connection grows through silence, habit, coffee, corrected spreadsheets, small offerings. The attraction is not declared. It accumulates through repeated gestures.
Engagement function:
This chapter must make the reader understand why both of them could mistake the arrangement for tenderness.
Ending pressure:
The chocolate remains in his drawer. He does not eat it. He does not throw it away.

Chapter 04 - The First Breach
Target valence: +2
Target tension: 5
Emotional movement:
From office protection into life protection.
Thomas sees Olesya's marginal position more clearly during the Sumgait trip. He pays the utility bill. The system crosses from work into home.
Engagement function:
The reader should feel the boundary move. This is the first real point of no return, but it should not announce itself.
Ending pressure:
Olesya holds the receipt as if it is proof of something larger than electricity.

Chapter 05 - Anya
Target valence: +3
Target tension: 5
Emotional movement:
From provision into domestic substitution.
Thomas enters Olesya's apartment and meets Anya. He sees the home Olesya is trying to maintain and supplies the missing capital. Sarah's parallel presence sharpens the inversion.
Engagement function:
The reader should feel the pull of Olesya's need and the chill of Thomas converting that need into procedure.
Ending pressure:
He leaves the apartment believing he has secured stability. Olesya remains inside, folding his money into the life she wants.

Chapter 06 - The Tether
Target valence: +2
Target tension: 6
Emotional movement:
From departure into deferred attachment.
Thomas leaves Baku. Olesya says he will return. He treats the envelope as severance; she treats it as continuity.
Engagement function:
This is the emotional hinge of Part I. The reader must understand that two different contracts have been created in the same gesture.
Ending pressure:
In the air, he asks for receipts. On the ground, she files one carefully.


PART II: EXPANSION - DISTANCE BEGINS

Chapter 07 - The Failed Meeting
Target valence: 0
Target tension: 6
Emotional movement:
From renewed contact into blocked movement.
The Istanbul plan fails because of Olesya's exit restriction. Sarah's mobility and Olesya's restriction become structural opposites.
Engagement function:
The story question sharpens: can Thomas maintain two loves when one exists inside official life and the other cannot move freely at all?
Ending pressure:
He says they will find a solution. She hangs up.

Chapter 08 - The Transfer
Target valence: +1
Target tension: 5
Emotional movement:
From crisis into temporary relief.
The first major wire transfer establishes the mechanism. Thomas does not reply with feeling. He replies with money.
Engagement function:
This chapter should feel almost satisfying. A problem arises. The system solves it. The trap is that the solution works.
Ending pressure:
The transfer receipt becomes more intimate than any message he could send.

Chapter 09 - Infrastructure
Target valence: +2
Target tension: 5
Emotional movement:
From emergency response into identity.
Thomas becomes Olesya and Anya's primary infrastructure. Anya's hospital crisis gives him the strongest proof yet that his system can produce safety.
Engagement function:
The reader should feel the seductive power of competence. Thomas saves someone. The moral danger is hidden inside the usefulness.
Ending pressure:
He checks Sarah's itinerary, then checks the exchange rate for Olesya's bill.

Chapter 10 - The Stipend
Target valence: +1
Target tension: 6
Emotional movement:
From support into subscription.
The monthly payment begins. The relationship becomes a fixed outgoing. Sarah notices his personal account dropping.
Engagement function:
The tension comes from normality. The arrangement is no longer a crisis. It is budgeted.
Ending pressure:
Olesya's birthday is answered with a transfer screenshot.

Chapter 11 - Perfect Stasis
Target valence: +2
Target tension: 6
Emotional movement:
From routine into ideological confidence.
The system appears to work. Anya advances. Sarah's life flourishes. Thomas holds both women in separate compartments and mistakes compartmentalisation for equality.
Engagement function:
This should be the highest functioning point of the system. The reader should almost believe him.
Ending pressure:
Olesya celebrates Anya's success with cake. Thomas sleeps well.


PART III: CONTAINMENT - IRAQ

Chapter 12 - Exposure Risk
Target valence: 0
Target tension: 7
Emotional movement:
From separation into compression.
Sarah joins him in Iraq. Physical proximity threatens the hidden system. An email from Olesya nearly exposes the financial arrangement.
Engagement function:
The reader should feel the walls narrowing. The problem is not guilt. It is containment.
Ending pressure:
He moves Olesya to the secure app. The system becomes smaller and more secretive.

Chapter 13 - Two Bunkers
Target valence: -1
Target tension: 8
Emotional movement:
From controlled secrecy into simultaneous crisis.
Mortar fire traps Thomas with Sarah while Olesya and Anya are locked out of their flat in Baku. His two realities demand protection at the same time.
Engagement function:
This is the first chapter where the system cannot be emotionally neutral. The reader should feel the impossible simultaneity.
Ending pressure:
He clears the bank block after the siren ends, but the delay has entered the system.

Chapter 14 - Lockdown
Target valence: -2
Target tension: 8
Emotional movement:
From crisis management into exhaustion.
The global lockdown compresses everything. Sarah is trapped. Olesya needs more. Financial traces must be hidden. Thomas becomes the single point of failure.
Engagement function:
The chapter should feel airless. Every solution makes the container smaller.
Ending pressure:
He protects two lives by making himself less visible in both.

Chapter 15 - The Black Box
Target valence: -2
Target tension: 8
Emotional movement:
From provision with visibility into provision without knowledge.
Olesya's messages shrink into blunt demands. Thomas prefers the reduced detail because it lets him avoid imagining the life he funds.
Engagement function:
This is a moral chill chapter. The reader sees that his love now functions better when the beloved is less human to him.
Ending pressure:
He sends the urgent 800 and waits for thanks that never arrives.


PART IV: SYSTEM FAILURE - DELHI

Chapter 16 - The Jammed Flow
Target valence: -3
Target tension: 8
Emotional movement:
From strained operation into failed transfer.
The monthly payment bounces. Banking compliance demands a documented relationship. Thomas cannot provide one.
Engagement function:
This chapter turns the abstract flaw into concrete failure. Love that cannot be named cannot be processed by the systems he depends on.
Ending pressure:
Olesya writes that they are in the dark. Thomas talks to Sarah about Delhi housing.

Chapter 17 - Bypass
Target valence: -1
Target tension: 8
Emotional movement:
From panic into ugly restoration.
Thomas finds a new route through a European platform. Money moves again, but the illusion of effortless provision is broken.
Engagement function:
The reader gets relief, but it should feel contaminated. The bridge is restored with visible bolts and wires.
Ending pressure:
Olesya says thank you without warmth.

Chapter 18 - Received
Target valence: -3
Target tension: 8
Emotional movement:
From restored mechanism into human stripping.
The platform fails. Crypto brokers and cash-out routes replace intimacy. Messages reduce to confirmation.
Engagement function:
The chapter should feel procedural in a frightening way. The language of love has become settlement language.
Ending pressure:
Thomas stares at one word: Received.

Chapter 19 - Hospital
Target valence: -4
Target tension: 9
Emotional movement:
From silence into medical crisis.
Olesya is hospitalised. Anya contacts Thomas. The debt collectors have consumed the money meant for stability.
Engagement function:
This is the revelation that Thomas treated symptoms, not structure. The system was never solving the thing that mattered.
Ending pressure:
He sends more money directly to Anya and understands that he is funding a tragedy.

Chapter 20 - Extraction Logic
Target valence: -2
Target tension: 8
Emotional movement:
From damage control into grand solution.
Olesya returns home. The debt is not cleared. Thomas concludes that remote maintenance has failed and begins designing the Norway extraction.
Engagement function:
This chapter gives the reader a dangerous lift. A new solution appears. It should feel both hopeful and wrong.
Ending pressure:
He researches immigration law as if love can be solved by sponsorship criteria.


PART V: FINAL ATTEMPTS - NORWAY

Chapter 21 - The Wall
Target valence: -3
Target tension: 9
Emotional movement:
From clean Norwegian order into total administrative blockage.
Thomas tries to create a job and visa route for Olesya. The debt appears in full scale. Company compliance kills the plan.
Engagement function:
Norway should feel brutally clean. Its systems work perfectly, which is precisely why his hidden arrangement cannot survive them.
Ending pressure:
He types that he cannot clear the debt and the visa is cancelled.

Chapter 22 - Project
Target valence: -4
Target tension: 9
Emotional movement:
From failed authority into rejection.
Olesya tells him he offers solutions that do not fix the real problem. She refuses his attempt to engineer her life and moves north with Anya.
Engagement function:
This is the clearest articulation of the novel's wound, but it must still arrive through behaviour and message fragments rather than speechifying.
Ending pressure:
His message remains sent, not read.

Chapter 23 - Funding An Absence
Target valence: -4
Target tension: 8
Emotional movement:
From active relationship into surveillance of absence.
Transfers continue, but acknowledgement stops. Thomas hires an investigator and sees the life his money did not save.
Engagement function:
The reader should feel the horror of knowledge without agency. He knows where she is, and that knowledge gives him no real power.
Ending pressure:
He deletes the file and decides on one final intervention.


PART VI: COLLAPSE

Chapter 24 - The Edge
Target valence: -5
Target tension: 10
Emotional movement:
From final hope into active refusal.
Thomas arranges Istanbul. Olesya agrees, reaches the crossing point, pays the fee, and turns back.
Engagement function:
This is the emotional climax. She does not fail to arrive. She chooses not to enter the fragment he has built.
Ending pressure:
He types why, knowing she will not answer.

Chapter 25 - Just Access
Target valence: -5 moving to -3
Target tension: 7
Emotional movement:
From absolute block into cold residual access.
The secure app returns user not found. The money route closes. Months later, he watches Olesya in Anya's TikTok under a false name.
Engagement function:
The ending should not provide catharsis. It should provide a final form of contact emptied of relationship. He has access, but no place.
Ending pressure:
He does not like. He does not comment. He watches from a name that is not his.

WORD-COUNT GUIDANCE FOR 5,000-WORD CHAPTERS

Each chapter should carry:
1,000 to 1,300 words of scene grounding and immediate action
1,200 to 1,500 words of interpersonal or logistical complication
1,000 to 1,200 words of pressure escalation
700 to 900 words of consequence, misalignment, or incomplete resolution
300 to 500 words of quiet ending pressure

Avoid padding through explanation. Expansion should come from:
More embodied scene work
More precise object detail
More pressure in decisions
More contrast between Sarah's visible life and Olesya's hidden one
More financial and bureaucratic friction
More withheld dialogue
More incomplete endings


FINAL SHAPE

The emotional promise of the novel is not that love fails because Thomas is cruel.

It fails because he builds a system that can move money, solve paperwork, hide transfers, create jobs, cross borders, and locate people, but cannot give Olesya the one thing she wants: a whole life.

The valence must therefore end below zero, but not in spectacle.

It ends with access.

Under another name.
