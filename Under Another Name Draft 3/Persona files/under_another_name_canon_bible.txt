UNDER ANOTHER NAME — CANON BIBLE (UPDATED)

TITLE:
Under Another Name

CORE CONCEPT:
A duality novel about a man who believes he can love two women equally and unconditionally, sustaining both lives through systems (money, logistics, distance), until the structure collapses when one person stops participating.

CENTRAL THEME:
Unconditional Love vs Structural Reality

KEY LINE (THEMATIC):
The end had already happened. The block only confirmed it.

---

SETTING FRAMEWORK:
1. Baku, Azerbaijan — origin
2. Iraq (oil rigs) — containment
3. Delhi, India — system failure
4. Norway — final attempt and collapse

---

CHARACTERS:

PROTAGONIST:
- Male oil rig engineer
- Systems thinker (money, timing, logistics)
- Avoids confrontation
- Believes he can love two women equally
- Does not believe in one man–one woman structure
- Converts love into systems instead of choices

CORE TRUTH:
He believes in unconditional dual love,
but lives it through conditional systems.

---

FEMALE (OLESYA ANALOGUE):
- Ethnic Russian minority in Azerbaijan
- Socially marginalised (subtle, never explained)
- Financial instability
- Withdraws instead of confronting
- Has a daughter (teen → adult)

CORE DESIRE:
- To be a wife
- To manage a home
- To raise children
- To belong in a stable, singular life

CORE TRUTH:
She accepts fragments until she realises they will never become whole.

---

WIFE:
- Present in Iraq and later
- Not antagonistic
- Represents visible life and structure
- Travels frequently → financial pressure

CORE DESIRE:
- Independence
- Movement
- A life not confined to domestic expectation

CORE TRUTH:
She occupies the role Olesya desires,
but does not embody that life.

---

STRUCTURAL INVERSION (CRITICAL):

Olesya wants to be the wife.
The wife lives the position, but not the desire.

The protagonist loves both equally,
but equality without structure becomes fragmentation.

---

STRUCTURE (25 CHAPTERS):

PART I — ORIGIN (Baku)
- Hired for work (incompetent)
- He corrects everything silently
- Attraction through proximity
- He compensates beyond work
- Ends: he leaves, she says he will return

PART II — EXPANSION
- Failed travel due to debt
- He begins sending money
- Relationship becomes financial system
- Pattern: problem → money

PART III — CONTAINMENT (Iraq)
- Wife arrives
- Restricted movement
- Dual life intensifies
- Her messages thin
- He maintains both

PART IV — SYSTEM FAILURE (Delhi)
- Money transfer fails
- Her situation deteriorates
- Messages reduce
- He restores payments too late

PART V — FINAL ATTEMPTS (Norway)
- Tries to bring her
- Debt blocks movement
- Pays debts → more appear
- Stops escalating effort

PART VI — COLLAPSE
- She agrees to come
- He buys ticket
- She withdraws
- She doesn’t arrive
- He is blocked

---

FINAL SCENE:
He watches her TikTok under a fake name.
No interaction.
No contact.

FINAL LINES:
Just access.

Under another name.

---

STYLE RULES:
- No emotional exposition
- No moral judgement
- No dramatic confrontation
- Show through behaviour
- Use objects and routine
- Silence carries meaning

---

MOTIFS:
- Systems working/failing
- Repetition of behaviour
- Unused objects (tickets, food)
- Distance instead of resolution

---

GOVERNING PARADOX:

He believes love can be equal.
Reality demands it be chosen.

---

FINAL TRUTH:

No one is wrong.
But their truths cannot exist in the same system.
