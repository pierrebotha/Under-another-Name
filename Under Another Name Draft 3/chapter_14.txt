The global lockdown accelerated the compression.

Flights out of Basra ceased entirely. Sarah was trapped in the compound. For Thomas, the dual lives were forced into the same pressurized container. 

In Baku, the situation was severe. 

*The grocery stores are empty,* Olesya messaged him. *The prices have tripled.*

*I will increase the transfer,* Thomas replied from the rig's control room. 

The financial demands escalated. He was funding an entire existence through a crisis. The secrecy required immense energy. 

Thomas and Sarah were watching a film. Sarah paused it.

"The London office wants to consolidate the offshore accounts," she said. "They asked for our joint tax declarations."

Thomas felt a knot in his stomach. The joint declarations were clean, but compiling them required reviewing all capital flow. 

"I can handle the compilation," Thomas said. 

"I can do it," Sarah offered. "Just give me the login for the archive."

"It's fine, Sarah," Thomas said sharply. "It's faster if I do it."

Sarah looked at him. "Alright."

Late that night, Thomas spent three hours scrubbing his digital financial history. He built a firewall of data to protect the system. 

He checked the secure app. 

*Anya is frightened. The news is very bad here. She asks if you are safe.*

He was safe, barricaded in a fortified compound, protecting his wife's assets while insulating Olesya from starvation. He was the single point of failure for two entirely separate realities. 

*Tell Anya I am safe,* he typed. *The money for the private clinic is reserved.*

He was managing the logistics of survival on two fronts. The effort to keep them separate was exhausting, but allowing them to collide was unthinkable. It would shatter the paradox he lived by.
