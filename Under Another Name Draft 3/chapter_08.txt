The silence lasted for nearly two months. Thomas finalized his deployment preparations for Iraq. Sarah was planning to join him there eventually; the company maintained secure, high-end compounds for senior staff. 

On a Tuesday morning in late May, an email arrived to his personal address. 

*Thomas. The landlord is threatening eviction. Anya's school fees are due on Friday. I have nowhere else to ask. I am sorry. - Olesya.*

It was a simple statement of system failure. 

He opened his international transfer portal. He calculated the sum required to cover rent for three months and the school fees. He clicked 'Transfer'. 

He did not reply to the email. The transfer itself was the reply. It was a functional response to a logistical crisis.

Three days later, he received a text message. 

*The money is here. I paid the landlord. I paid the school. Thank you, Thomas. You saved us.*

*Make sure you get a receipt from the landlord,* he typed back.

*I have it. I am looking for work every day.*

The pattern was set. When a problem arose—a medical bill, a sudden hike in utility prices—she would notify him. Thomas would read the message, calculate the required sum, and initiate the transfer. 

He justified it to himself as an expression of his unconditional love, converted into a manageable logistical system. He could not be there to physically maintain the space around her, so he provided the capital for her to maintain it herself. 

He did not discuss this with Sarah. The funds came from his separate account. 

As the weeks turned into months, the transfers morphed into a baseline subsidy. He was, effectively, paying a subscription to maintain her stability. 

In London, his life was built on parity and mutual competence with Sarah. Sarah was his equal, a partner in a joint venture. But in the shadow of Baku, he was the sole architect of survival. He provided the structure that Olesya desperately craved. 

He loved the parity with Sarah, and he loved the absolute dependency of Olesya. He held both realities in perfect, isolated equilibrium. He did not see the inherent fragility of equality without proximity, or dependency sustained only by capital.
