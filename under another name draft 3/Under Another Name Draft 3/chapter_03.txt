The winter over the Caspian was brutal. Inside the office, Olesya’s presence was a necessary hum against Thomas's concentration. 

Proximity breeds intimacy through the observation of microscopic habits. Thomas knew Olesya took exactly three steps to cross from her desk to his door. He knew she drank water only in the early afternoon. 

Late one Thursday evening, Thomas was reviewing a structural integrity report. He heard the scrape of a chair. Olesya was still at her desk, manually entering serial numbers into a spreadsheet. 

Thomas pulled up a chair next to her desk. He took control of the mouse.

"Watch," he said. He used OCR software to extract the data, taking four minutes. 

She stared at the completed spreadsheet, looking defeated. "I am very slow."

"You don't know the tools," Thomas corrected.

She spoke again, barely above a whisper. "Rauf says I am useless. He thinks I do not hear."

"Rauf is concerned with metrics," Thomas said carefully. "As long as the outputs are correct, his opinion is irrelevant."

"But you fix the outputs," she said. She looked at him. "I know you fix my files, Thomas."

The omission of his title hung in the air. 

"The work gets done," he said. 

"Why?" she asked.

"Because it is efficient," Thomas said. 

He went back into his office. The next morning, a small, foil-wrapped chocolate lay beside his coffee. He placed it in his top drawer.

The attraction grew in the negative space. It was an alignment of needs. Olesya desired the structure of a provided life; she wanted to manage a home, to belong entirely to a singular, stable unit. Thomas provided the stability, but he did so while maintaining his parallel life with Sarah. 

He did not view this as a contradiction. In his mind, the structural reality of his marriage to Sarah—the shared assets, the mutual independence—did not negate his deep, protective attachment to Olesya. He believed unconditional love could be distributed equally, provided the systems supporting each life were maintained flawlessly.

The silence between them in the Baku office was the sound of that system calibrating.
