The final attempt was a desperate breach of logic. 

He could not go to Azerbaijan. He chose Istanbul. 

He paid a border official via the investigator to facilitate an undocumented crossing into Georgia. He booked flights from Tbilisi to Istanbul and reserved a suite. 

He compiled the itinerary and sent it to Olesya on the secure app. 

*I have arranged a route out. It is safe. Meet me in Istanbul on the 14th.*

He waited. For three days, the message sat unread. 

Then, the indicator changed. *Read.*

*Thomas. I cannot.*

*You must,* he replied. *It is the only way. I have the funds waiting in Istanbul.*

*Anya will not leave the village. She is angry with you.*

*Then come alone. Just for a few days.*

The silence was agonizing. 

*Okay,* the reply finally came. *I will try the crossing.*

He informed Sarah he had a supply chain meeting in Turkey. He flew to Istanbul on the 13th. He laid out the cash in the hotel suite. 

The 14th arrived. Her flight landed at noon. 

At 1:00 PM, he sent a message. *Are you at the airport?*

The message remained marked as 'Sent'. 

At 3:00 PM, he contacted the investigator. *Confirm the subject crossed the border.*

The investigator replied an hour later. *The guard confirms she arrived at the crossing point. She paid the secondary fee. But she did not cross. She turned back.*

Thomas stared at the message. 

She had gone to the edge. She had looked at the route out, and she had chosen to turn back. She desired a home, a singular life, not an illicit meeting in a hotel room funded by abstract capital. She rejected the fragment. 

*Why?* Thomas typed, knowing she would not answer. 

He had failed to calculate the point at which the human element rejects the logic of the system. She had not failed to arrive. She had actively chosen not to.
