The European platform worked for two months before it flagged the transactions. 

Thomas was at a corporate dinner with Sarah. An automated email informed him his account was suspended and requested identity verification from the recipient.

He closed the email. He could not ask Olesya for her passport. 

He found a complex route involving cryptocurrency and a local cash-out broker in Baku. It was messy and violated his preference for abstraction. 

*I need you to meet someone,* he messaged Olesya. *He will give you the funds in cash.*

*I will not meet a strange man with cash, Thomas. It is not safe.*

*It is the only way. The digital routes are blocked.*

*Then I will find another way here.*

*What other way?* he asked. 

She did not reply. She was refusing the resource because the delivery mechanism was unacceptable. 

He tried again three days later. 

*I found a different broker. He will deposit the cash directly into your bank account.*

*Okay.*

He executed the transaction. Nearly forty percent of the capital was lost in transit fees. 

*The deposit is complete. Confirm receipt.*

*Received.*

There was no update on Anya. The messages became a grim routine. He initiated the transfer. She confirmed receipt. 

*Received.*

*Received.*

The relationship was stripped of its human element. It was a logistical exercise in bypassing regulations. He was no longer funding a life; he was funding a mechanism. 

He sat in his company car in Delhi traffic. He looked at the word *Received*. 

He believed he was sustaining love through this mechanism, but the reality was that equality without proximity had become fragmentation. The system was eating itself.
