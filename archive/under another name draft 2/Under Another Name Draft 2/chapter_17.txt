Delhi was a sensory overload of heat and noise. The company installed them in a spacious apartment in Vasant Vihar. Sarah adapted quickly, hiring a driver and integrating into the local expat networks. 

Thomas's primary focus was resolving the banking lock. 

He dialed the international support number. The wait was long. 

"The account remains frozen pending satisfactory documentation of the payee relationship," the compliance officer stated. 

"It is personal support," Thomas reiterated. 

"Without a formalized remittance structure or familial documentation, we cannot release the hold."

"How do I resolve it?"

"Submit an affidavit of support, detailing the relationship."

Thomas hung up. He could not submit an affidavit. The system of dual love he had constructed relied entirely on its illegibility to the formal structures of his life. To document Olesya was to invite the collapse of his marriage, not because he believed he was betraying Sarah, but because he knew the world—and Sarah—demanded singular devotion.

He opened the secure app. There were five new messages. 

*We sold the television to pay the electricity.*

*Anya is staying at a friend's house. It is too cold here.*

*Thomas, please tell me what is happening.*

*Are you okay?*

*I do not know what to do.*

The progression from logistical updates to panic. He had caused this. 

He spent two days researching informal value transfer systems. He finally identified a digital payment platform in Europe. He linked a high-limit credit card and initiated a transfer. The fees were exorbitant. 

He messaged Olesya. 

*The banking block is bypassed. I have sent three months' worth. It should clear tomorrow.*

*I see it pending. Thank you.*

There was no joy in her message. It was the flat acknowledgement of a worker confirming receipt. The illusion of effortless provision was gone. She now knew the structure was fragile. She realized the fragments of care he provided were not accumulating into a whole life; they were just fragments, desperately patched together to delay a collapse.
