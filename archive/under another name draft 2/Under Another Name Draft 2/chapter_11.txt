By the fourth year, the arrangement had achieved perfect stasis. 

The transfers to Baku occurred with automated precision. The messages from Olesya arrived regularly. 

*Anya is looking at universities. She wants to study engineering.*

*She has the aptitude,* he replied. *I will cover the tuition.*

He felt a distant sense of proprietary pride. He was the silent architect of Anya's upward mobility. 

*She asked about you today. She asked why you send the money.*

Thomas stared at the screen. 

*What did you tell her?*

*I told her you were a friend from the old office. That you understood our situation.*

It was an acceptable answer. 

Sarah was reviewing architectural plans for a renovation in London. "They want to push the kitchen extension out. It increases the budget."

"Approve it," Thomas said. "The return on investment will cover the outlay."

Sarah nodded. "You're always so decisive with capital expenditure."

"It's just math," Thomas said. 

He avoided examining what the arrangement actually was. He never used the word 'relationship' in his own mind. 

He believed he loved both women equally. Sarah had the title, the social recognition, the shared assets. Olesya had his provision, his protection, the quiet dedication of his resources. 

Anya graduated secondary school and was accepted into the engineering program. 

*We celebrated tonight,* Olesya messaged him. *We bought a cake. Anya says thank you.*

*Congratulations,* he replied. 

He had secured the outcome. The system was functioning perfectly. He went to sleep in the quiet, climate-controlled compound, the mechanism humming silently in the background of his life, completely unaware that Olesya was assembling these fragments of support, waiting for the day they would form a whole.
