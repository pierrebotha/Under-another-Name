The end of the Iraq deployment was abrupt. The company relocated them to a regional hub in Delhi. 

Amidst the chaos of packing, Thomas monitored the secure app. 

*Transfer did not clear,* Olesya messaged on a Thursday. 

Thomas checked his portal. The monthly transfer had bounced back. A regulatory hold. 

He initiated a secure chat with the bank. 

*Your account is under review due to the volume of international wires to a high-risk jurisdiction,* the automated representative informed him. *Please provide documentation establishing the payee relationship.*

The firewall was failing. He had no legal standing with Olesya. 

*I cannot clear this immediately,* he messaged Olesya. *Banking issue. I will fix it from Delhi.*

*I need it now, Thomas. They will turn off the electricity.*

It was the same problem they had faced years ago, but now he was separated by locked borders and a frozen financial mechanism. 

*I cannot send it,* he replied. 

*Anya cannot study in the dark.*

Thomas did not respond. He boarded the chartered flight to Delhi. He felt a profound operational failure. 

He connected to the plane's Wi-Fi and opened the app. 

*Thomas. Are you there? We are in the dark.*

He did not reply. He compressed the problem, pushing it down. 

"The company housing in Delhi is supposed to be excellent," Thomas said to Sarah. "We should look into hiring local staff."

"It will be a nice change," Sarah said. 

"It will be efficient," Thomas agreed. 

The old system was broken. The capital flow that sustained his belief in unconditional dual love was jammed. He would have to build a new one.
