# Novel Outline

## PART I — ORIGIN (Chapters 1–6) — Baku
Chapter 1: Arrival in Baku. Rig work. Meets Elena (the Russian minority woman). Hires her for admin/logistics.
Chapter 2: Elena's incompetence at tasks. He quietly corrects spreadsheets, fixes errors.
Chapter 3: Routine establishes. Coffee, silent corrections. Attraction through proximity.
Chapter 4: Compensating for her failures bleeds into life. He pays a bill for her quietly. Subtle social tension of her being a Russian minority in post-Soviet Baku.
Chapter 5: Meeting her daughter briefly. The unspoken boundaries.
Chapter 6: His rotation ends. Packing. She says, "You will come back." He leaves.

## PART II — EXPANSION (Chapters 7–11) — Distance Begins
Chapter 7: Back home / elsewhere. First attempt to meet in a third country. Fails due to her exit restrictions (debt).
Chapter 8: The first wire transfer. Establishing the system.
Chapter 9: The pattern forms. Problem arises (rent, daughter's school) -> He wires money.
Chapter 10: Distance normalizes. Occasional calls. The relationship becomes the maintenance of her stability.
Chapter 11: Years pass in this pattern. He avoids examining what it is.

## PART III — CONTAINMENT (Chapters 12–16) — Iraq
Chapter 12: Transferred to Iraq. Dust, heat, compounds. Wife arrives to live in the secure compound.
Chapter 13: Physical confinement. He manages work, wife, and the hidden system.
Chapter 14: COVID-like restrictions hit. Movement locked down. The dual life compresses into his phone and late-night wire transfers.
Chapter 15: Messages from Elena begin to thin. She stops explaining the problems, just implies the need.
Chapter 16: He compresses both lives without choosing. Avoiding wife's questions about finances.

## PART IV — SYSTEM FAILURE (Chapters 17–20) — Delhi
Chapter 17: Relocated to Delhi. Wife remains or moves with him. Banking regulations change. He cannot send money easily.
Chapter 18: The system breaks. Wires bounce back. Her situation deteriorates.
Chapter 19: Messages shift from emotional/updates to purely practical, then minimal. Panic disguised as silence.
Chapter 20: He finally routes the money through an intermediary. The mechanism is restored, but the relationship has shifted irreversibly.

## PART V — FINAL ATTEMPTS (Chapters 21–23) — Norway
Chapter 21: Moved to Norway. Cold, structured, systems work perfectly here. He tries to bring her to him on a visa.
Chapter 22: Debt issues block her movement again. The background debt is revealed to be much larger.
Chapter 23: He attempts to clear it, but more hidden debt appears. He stops escalating his effort. The limit of the system is reached.

## PART VI — COLLAPSE (Chapters 24–25)
Chapter 24: She agrees to come anyway. He buys the flight ticket. She becomes distant, stops replying regularly.
Chapter 25: The arrival date. She does not arrive. He attempts contact. Fails. Blocked everywhere. The end had already happened; the block only confirmed it.
