The security situation in southern Iraq deteriorated. Thomas was relocated to the fortified compound near Majnoon. Sarah joined him. 

The compound was a bubble of western infrastructure surrounded by blast walls. Sarah quickly integrated, managing her remote work and the expat social committee. 

Thomas found the confinement claustrophobic. The physical boundaries were absolute. 

He was reviewing schematics when Sarah walked in. 

"The Wi-Fi keeps dropping," she said. "I need to upload these reports."

"Use my laptop," Thomas said. He had not closed his email client. 

Sarah plugged in her drive. An email notification popped up. 

*Subject: Anya's university fees - Update*

"Who is Olesya?" Sarah asked, her tone casual.

Thomas turned slowly. The system was exposed. 

"An old contact," Thomas said evenly. "From the Baku project. She handles local administrative overflow."

"She's asking about university fees?"

"I occasionally consult on the logistical costs."

"Tell her to route the requests through HR," Sarah said, focusing on her upload. 

"I don't handle operational expenses from my personal account," Thomas lied smoothly. 

"Right. Upload is done." She left the room. 

Thomas opened the email. 

*Thomas. The university is demanding the next semester's tuition upfront. The amount is higher than usual. I am sorry to ask.*

He replied quickly. *Understood. I will adjust the transfer. Do not email this address again. Use the secure messaging app.*

He deleted the email. He had to compress the system. He moved the financial transactions to a VPN app on his phone. 

When he joined Sarah in the mess hall, she was discussing her next trip to London. She occupied the role Olesya desired—the wife, the partner—but she did not embody the domesticity of it. She was independent, mobile. 

Thomas looked at her. He was managing two entirely separate realities. He believed he was providing equally for both, maintaining the structure of his marriage and the survival of Olesya. But the proximity within the compound was thinning the walls between them. The system required more robust containment protocols.
