As the lockdown stretched, the communication from Baku changed. 

Olesya’s messages became shorter, stripped of context. 

*Need 500.* 

Thomas received this message in the operations center. The omission of detail irritated him. He provided the capital; she provided the operational reason. 

He replied: *What is the 500 for?*

*Just need it. Please.*

He transferred the money. The pattern persisted. 

*Need extra for winter clothes.*

*Anya's laptop broke.*

He provided the funds each time without pressing for details. He realized he preferred this stripped-down communication. The detailed messages required him to visualize their life. The blunt requests required only a numerical response. It allowed him to maintain the illusion of provision without the burden of emotional reality. 

In the compound, the isolation was taking a toll on Sarah. 

"We need to get out of here, Thomas," she said. "As soon as the airspace opens, we need to request a transfer. Norway, maybe."

"I will speak to the regional director," Thomas agreed. 

"You seem fine," Sarah said. "Doesn't this bother you? You're so detached sometimes. It's like you're managing a simulation."

Thomas did not reply. He was managing two simulations. 

Late that night, his phone vibrated. 

*Need 800. Urgent.*

He transferred the 800. 

*Sent.*

He waited for the usual 'Thank you'. It did not arrive. 

The silence that followed felt like a void. He did not know what the 800 was for. He had lost visibility into the operational details of the Baku node. 

He was no longer stabilizing a situation; he was pouring resources into a black box. But he could not stop. Stopping meant acknowledging that his system of equal love was failing. He would continue to fund the black box, because the alternative was the collapse of his entire ideological structure.
