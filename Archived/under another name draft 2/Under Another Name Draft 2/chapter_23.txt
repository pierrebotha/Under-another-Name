Winter arrived in Stavanger with brutal efficiency. 

Thomas's life had narrowed. He went to the office. He returned home to the glass house. He spoke to Sarah about investments. Every evening, he checked the secure messaging app. 

The message remained unread. The monthly transfers continued to disappear into the ether. He was funding an absence. 

Sarah suggested a holiday. "Let's go somewhere warm. You haven't been engaged for months, Thomas. Are you having an affair?"

An affair had rules, conventions. He had none of those things. He had a ledger of wire transfers to a woman hiding in a village he couldn't name. 

"No," Thomas said. "I am not having an affair."

"Then what is it?" she asked.

"It's just the work," he lied. 

The next day, Thomas initiated a contact protocol he had never used before. He hired a private investigator in Baku to locate Olesya and Anya without making contact. 

Three weeks later, the investigator sent a secure file. 

The report confirmed they were living in a decaying village near the Russian border. Anya had withdrawn from university and was working in a bakery. Olesya's health was poor. 

Thomas opened the photographs. 

One showed Anya carrying flour. The other showed Olesya outside a cinderblock house, hanging laundry. She looked frail. 

The reality of her existence stared back at him. He had not saved her. He had merely prolonged the collapse, tying her to a system that eventually failed her. 

He deleted the file. He knew where she was, but the knowledge provided no solution. It only confirmed the absolute failure of his methods. 

He had to attempt one final intervention. He had to close the distance.
