Stavanger was a city of sharp angles and clean lines. Thomas and Sarah moved into a glass-fronted house overlooking the fjord. Sarah integrated quickly into the structured corporate life. 

Thomas initiated the extraction plan. He leveraged his position to create a specialized administrative role in the Stavanger office, requiring bilingual Russian-English capabilities. He initiated the visa process for Olesya. 

He messaged her. *I have arranged a job for you in Norway. You and Anya will relocate.*

It was a directive. It was the only way to prove his unconditional love could exist in a singular reality.

*Thomas. I cannot move. The exit restriction. My passport is still blocked.*

*Tell me the number required to clear the block,* he demanded. 

The number arrived. It was staggering. Over a decade of punitive interest and bureaucratic penalties. It was a sum that would require liquidating joint assets with Sarah. It would require active fraud against his marriage. 

He was at the absolute limit of the system. 

Two days later, a compliance officer knocked on his door. 

"The background check on the candidate, Olesya... we ran into a flag," the officer said. "She has a massive, outstanding debt with collection agencies in Baku. Company policy strictly prohibits sponsoring visas for individuals with unresolved debt of this magnitude."

Thomas looked at the officer. The system was dead. The corporate machinery he had tried to use to save her had exposed the fatal flaw. 

"Cancel the requisition," Thomas said. 

When the officer left, Thomas turned his chair to face the window. The debt was a wall he could not breach. His paradox was collapsing; his love was conditional on his ability to fund the structure, and the structure had failed.

He opened the secure app. 

*I cannot clear the debt,* he typed. *The visa is cancelled.*

He had severed the line.
