# AUDIT REPORT: Chapters 1-24 vs Canon Bible
## UNDER ANOTHER NAME

---

## CANON BIBLE REQUIREMENTS (Summary)

**Core Concept:** A man believes he can love two women equally through systems (money, logistics, distance) until structure collapses when one person stops participating.

**Central Theme:** Unconditional Love vs Structural Reality

**Key Line:** "The end had already happened. The block only confirmed it."

**Setting Framework:**
1. Baku, Azerbaijan — origin
2. Iraq (oil rigs) — containment
3. Delhi, India — system failure
4. Norway — final attempt and collapse

**Characters:**
- **Protagonist (Thomas):** Male oil rig engineer, systems thinker, avoids confrontation, believes he can love two women equally, converts love into systems instead of choices
- **Olesya:** Ethnic Russian minority in Azerbaijan, socially marginalized, financial instability, withdraws instead of confronting, has daughter (teen → adult), CORE DESIRE: to be a wife, manage a home, raise children, belong in stable singular life
- **Wife (Sarah):** Present in Iraq and later, not antagonistic, represents visible life and structure, travels frequently, CORE DESIRE: independence, movement, life not confined to domestic expectation

**Structural Inversion (CRITICAL):**
- Olesya wants to be the wife
- The wife lives the position, but not the desire
- Protagonist loves both equally, but equality without structure becomes fragmentation

**Structure (25 Chapters):**
- PART I — ORIGIN (Baku): Hired for work (incompetent), he corrects everything silently, attraction through proximity, he compensates beyond work, ends: he leaves, she says he will return
- PART II — EXPANSION: Failed travel due to debt, he begins sending money, relationship becomes financial system, pattern: problem → money
- PART III — CONTAINMENT (Iraq): Wife arrives, restricted movement, dual life intensifies, her messages thin, he maintains both
- PART IV — SYSTEM FAILURE (Delhi): Money transfer fails, her situation deteriorates, messages reduce, he restores payments too late
- PART V — FINAL ATTEMPTS (Norway): Tries to bring her, debt blocks movement, pays debts → more appear, stops escalating effort
- PART VI — COLLAPSE: She agrees to come, he buys ticket, she withdraws, she doesn't arrive, he is blocked

**Final Scene:** He watches her TikTok under a fake name. No interaction. No contact. Final lines: "Just access. Under another name."

**Style Rules:**
- No emotional exposition
- No moral judgement
- No dramatic confrontation
- Show through behaviour
- Use objects and routine
- Silence carries meaning

**Motifs:**
- Systems working/failing
- Repetition of behaviour
- Unused objects (tickets, food)
- Distance instead of resolution

**Governing Paradox:** He believes love can be equal. Reality demands it be chosen.

**Final Truth:** No one is wrong. But their truths cannot exist in the same system.
